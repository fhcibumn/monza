Common Request at a Bar NYT Crossword

It's a lazy Sunday afternoon and you're at your favorite bar. You've got your drink in hand, and you're enjoying the chill vibe of the place. Suddenly, you hear someone ask the bartender for a drink that's not on the menu. You wonder what it could be. Well, wonder no more! Here are some common requests at a bar that you might come across while solving the New York Times crossword.

Neat

When someone asks for a drink "neat," it means they want it served straight up, without any ice or mixers. This is usually reserved for high-quality spirits that are meant to be sipped and savored, such as whiskey or tequila.

On the Rocks

If someone wants their drink "on the rocks," it means they want it served over ice. This is usually done for drinks that are too strong to be consumed straight up, or for those who prefer a colder drink. It's important to note that some drinks, such as martinis, are not typically served on the rocks.

With a Twist

When someone asks for their drink "with a twist," it means they want a small amount of citrus zest added to their drink. This is usually done for drinks such as martinis or gin and tonics, which benefit from the bright, fresh flavor of citrus.

Shaken, Not Stirred

The famous James Bond line aside, when someone asks for their drink "shaken, not stirred," it means they want their drink shaken with ice, rather than stirred. This is usually done for drinks that contain fruit juices or other mixers that need to be thoroughly mixed with the alcohol.

Make it a Double

If someone wants their drink "doubled," it means they want twice the amount of alcohol in their drink. This is usually done for drinks that are too weak for the person's taste, or for those who want to get a little extra buzz.
