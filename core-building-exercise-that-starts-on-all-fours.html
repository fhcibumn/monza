Core Building Exercise That Starts on All Fours

A Strong Core is Essential for Overall Health and Fitness

Many people think of a strong core as just having six-pack abs, but it's actually much more than that. Your core muscles are the foundation for all movement in your body, and having a strong core can improve your balance, stability, posture, and overall fitness level. One of the best ways to strengthen your core is by doing exercises that start on all fours.

The Bird Dog Exercise

The bird dog exercise is a great way to engage your core muscles while also working your back and glutes. To do it, start on all fours with your hands directly under your shoulders and your knees directly under your hips. Keeping your core engaged and your back flat, lift your right arm and left leg off the ground and extend them straight out in front of you and behind you, respectively. Hold for a few seconds, then lower back down and repeat on the other side.

Make sure to keep your movements slow and controlled, and focus on keeping your core muscles engaged throughout the entire exercise. As you get stronger, you can add repetitions and hold the position for longer periods of time.

The Plank with Shoulder Tap

The plank is a classic core exercise, but you can make it even more challenging by adding a shoulder tap. Start in a plank position with your forearms on the ground and your body in a straight line from your head to your heels. Keeping your core engaged, lift one hand off the ground and tap your opposite shoulder, then lower back down and repeat on the other side.

Make sure to keep your hips level and avoid twisting your body as you tap your shoulder. You can also try lifting one foot off the ground at the same time as you tap your shoulder, to add an extra challenge.

The Dead Bug Exercise

The dead bug is another great exercise for engaging your core muscles. Start on your back with your arms and legs in the air, and your knees bent at a 90-degree angle. Slowly lower your right arm and left leg towards the ground, keeping your core engaged and your back flat. Return to the starting position, then repeat on the other side.

Make sure to keep your movements slow and controlled, and avoid arching your back as you lower your arm and leg. You can also try extending both arms and legs at the same time, to make the exercise more challenging.

The Cat-Cow Stretch

The cat-cow stretch is a gentle exercise that can help improve mobility in your spine while also engaging your core muscles. Start on all fours with your hands directly under your shoulders and your knees directly under your hips. Inhale as you arch your back and lift your head and tailbone towards the ceiling (cow pose), then exhale as you round your spine and tuck your chin towards your chest (cat pose).

Repeat this movement slowly, focusing on engaging your core muscles and stretching your spine. You can also add in some lateral movement by moving your hips from side to side as you transition between cat and cow poses.
