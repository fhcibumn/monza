Are you planning to ship goods to or from Australia? Do you want to know what size of shipping container you need? The dimensions of shipping containers can be confusing, especially if you're new to international shipping. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will discuss the various shipping container dimensions in Australia and what they are used for.

20-Foot Shipping Container

The 20-foot shipping container is the most commonly used container in Australia. It measures 20 feet long, 8 feet wide, and 8 feet 6 inches high. This container can hold up to 33 cubic meters of cargo and can carry up to 28,000 kg of weight. It's ideal for shipping small to medium-sized goods, such as furniture, electronics, and consumer goods.

40-Foot Shipping Container

The 40-foot shipping container is another popular option in Australia. It measures 40 feet long, 8 feet wide, and 8 feet 6 inches high. This container can hold up to 67 cubic meters of cargo and can carry up to 28,000 kg of weight. It's ideal for shipping larger goods, such as vehicles, machinery, and oversized cargo.

High Cube Shipping Container

The high cube shipping container is similar to the 20-foot and 40-foot containers, but it's taller. It measures 20 feet or 40 feet long, 8 feet wide, and 9 feet 6 inches high. This container can hold up to 76 cubic meters of cargo and can carry up to 28,000 kg of weight. It's ideal for shipping goods that require extra height, such as construction equipment, boats, and RVs.

Refrigerated Shipping Container

The refrigerated shipping container, also known as a reefer, is a specialized container that is used for shipping temperature-sensitive goods. It comes in various sizes, including 20 feet, 40 feet, and 45 feet. This container is equipped with a refrigeration unit that can maintain a specific temperature range, typically between -25°C to +25°C. It's ideal for shipping perishable goods, such as fruits, vegetables, meat, and dairy products.

Open Top Shipping Container

The open top shipping container is another specialized container that is used for shipping oversized cargo. It measures 20 feet or 40 feet long, 8 feet wide, and 8 feet 6 inches or 9 feet 6 inches high. This container has an open top that can be covered with a tarpaulin. It's ideal for shipping goods that are too tall to fit in a standard container, such as machinery, construction equipment, and timber. In conclusion, choosing the right shipping container size and type is crucial for a successful international shipment. Make sure to consider the type and size of your goods, as well as any special requirements, such as temperature control or open top access. By understanding the various shipping container dimensions in Australia, you can ensure that your goods arrive at their destination safely and on time.
