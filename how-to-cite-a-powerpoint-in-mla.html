How to Cite a PowerPoint in MLA

If you are a student or a researcher, you must have come across PowerPoint presentations during your academic journey. PowerPoint presentations are an effective way to convey information in a visually appealing manner. However, when it comes to citing a PowerPoint presentation in your research paper, it can be a bit challenging. In this article, we will guide you on how to cite a PowerPoint presentation in MLA format.

What is MLA?

MLA stands for Modern Language Association. It is a citation style commonly used in the humanities, particularly in literature and language studies. MLA provides guidelines for citing sources in your research paper.

Step 1: Identify the Author and Title of the PowerPoint Presentation

The first step in citing a PowerPoint presentation in MLA is to identify the author and title of the presentation. This information can usually be found on the first slide of the presentation. If the author's name is not provided, you can use the username or screen name of the person who uploaded the presentation.

Step 2: Include the Presentation Type and Date

After identifying the author and title of the presentation, the next step is to include the presentation type and date. If the presentation is a standalone document, you can use the word "Presentation" as the presentation type. If the presentation is part of a larger collection, such as a conference or workshop, you can use the appropriate term, such as "Conference Presentation" or "Workshop Presentation". The date of the presentation should be included in day-month-year format.

Step 3: Provide the URL or DOI

The final step in citing a PowerPoint presentation in MLA format is to provide the URL or DOI. If the presentation is available online, include the URL. If the presentation has a DOI (Digital Object Identifier), use it instead of the URL. If you accessed the presentation through a database, include the name of the database and the accession number.

Conclusion

Citing a PowerPoint presentation in MLA format can be a bit tricky, but by following the above steps, you can ensure that your research paper is properly cited. Remember to always check with your instructor or supervisor for specific guidelines and requirements.
