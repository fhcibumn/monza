What To Do When You Have A Stomach Virus: Tips And Remedies

It's that time of the year again when stomach viruses are rampant. You wake up in the morning feeling fine, but by mid-day, your stomach feels like it's doing somersaults. You may experience nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach cramps. A stomach virus or gastroenteritis is a common ailment caused by a viral infection that affects the digestive system. It's highly contagious and can easily spread from one person to another. In this article, we'll discuss some useful tips and remedies for what to do when you have a stomach virus.

Stay Hydrated

One of the main complications of a stomach virus is dehydration. When you're vomiting or have diarrhea, your body loses fluids and essential minerals. It's crucial to drink plenty of fluids to avoid becoming dehydrated. Water is the best option, but you can also consume electrolyte drinks, coconut water, or clear broths. Avoid caffeine, alcohol, and sugary drinks as they can worsen dehydration.

Eat Light and Nutritious Foods

When you have a stomach virus, your body needs time to recover. It's best to avoid solid foods for the first few hours to allow your stomach to settle. After that, you can start with light and easily digestible foods such as crackers, toast, bananas, rice, and boiled potatoes. Avoid spicy, fatty, and greasy foods that can irritate your stomach and worsen your symptoms.

Rest and Relax

Resting is essential when you have a stomach virus. Your body needs time to recover, and overexerting yourself can make your symptoms worse. Take a break from work or school and stay home to rest. Try to reduce stress and anxiety as they can worsen your symptoms. You can practice meditation, deep breathing, or listen to calming music to help you relax.

Use Over-the-Counter Medications

Over-the-counter medications can help relieve some of your stomach virus symptoms. Antacids can help neutralize stomach acid and reduce heartburn and indigestion. Anti-diarrheal medications can help reduce diarrhea, but you should use them with caution as they can make your condition worse if you have an underlying infection. Pain relievers such as ibuprofen or acetaminophen can help reduce fever and pain.

Final Thoughts

A stomach virus can be uncomfortable and disruptive, but with the right care, you can recover quickly. It's essential to stay hydrated, eat light and nutritious foods, rest and relax, and use over-the-counter medications if necessary. If your symptoms persist or worsen, seek medical attention immediately. Remember to practice good hygiene by washing your hands frequently and avoid close contact with infected individuals.
