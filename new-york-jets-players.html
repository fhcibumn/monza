New York Jets Players in 2023

The New York Jets have had quite the offseason in 2023, with several new players joining the team. Fans are excited to see how these new additions will impact the team's performance this season. Let's take a closer look at some of the players on the roster.

1. Zach Wilson

Zach Wilson was the Jets' first-round pick in the 2023 NFL Draft, and he's expected to be the team's starting quarterback this season. Wilson played college football at BYU, where he threw for over 7,500 yards and 56 touchdowns in just two seasons. Jets fans are hoping he can bring that same level of success to the NFL.

Wilson has already impressed his coaches and teammates with his work ethic and leadership skills. He's also shown off his arm strength and accuracy in training camp, making some impressive throws during practice. If he can translate that to game day, the Jets could have a franchise quarterback on their hands.

Of course, there will be some growing pains as Wilson adjusts to the speed and complexity of the NFL game. But Jets fans are optimistic that he can lead the team to success in the coming years.

2. Carl Lawson

The Jets also made a big splash in free agency by signing defensive end Carl Lawson. Lawson spent the first four seasons of his career with the Cincinnati Bengals, where he recorded 20 sacks and 81 quarterback hits. He's known for his explosive first step and relentless pass-rushing ability.

The Jets are hoping Lawson can help improve a defense that struggled last season. With his ability to disrupt the quarterback, Lawson could make life easier for the rest of the defense by generating turnovers and forcing punts. He'll also be a valuable mentor to some of the younger players on the team.

Lawson has been impressive in training camp so far, showing off his speed and agility off the edge. If he can stay healthy and continue to produce at a high level, the Jets could have one of the best pass-rushers in the league.

3. Denzel Mims

Denzel Mims was a second-round pick for the Jets in the 2020 NFL Draft, but his rookie season was hampered by injuries. He only played in nine games, catching 23 passes for 357 yards and no touchdowns. But Mims has been healthy during training camp and is looking to make a bigger impact in his second season.

Mims has the size and speed to be a matchup nightmare for opposing defenses. At 6'3" and 207 pounds, he's a big target who can go up and get the ball in contested situations. He also has the speed to stretch the field and make big plays down the sideline.

If Mims can stay healthy and build a rapport with Wilson, he could be a breakout player for the Jets this season. He's shown flashes of his potential in training camp, making some impressive catches and showing off his athleticism.

C.J. Mosley was one of the Jets' big free-agent signings in 2019, but he missed almost all of his first season with the team due to injury. He opted out of the 2020 season due to COVID-19 concerns, so he hasn't played in a game since 2019. But Mosley is healthy and ready to make an impact in 2023.

Mosley is a four-time Pro Bowler and one of the best inside linebackers in the league when healthy. He's a smart and instinctive player who can make plays all over the field. He's also a leader on and off the field, and his presence should help improve a Jets defense that struggled last season.

If Mosley can stay healthy and return to his Pro Bowl form, he could be a game-changer for the Jets this season. He's already made an impression in training camp, showing off his athleticism and playmaking ability.
