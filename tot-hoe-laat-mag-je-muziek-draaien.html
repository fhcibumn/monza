A Guide to Noise Regulations in the Netherlands

Residential Areas

If you live in a residential area, the noise regulations are stricter than in commercial areas. The noise limit is 35 decibels during the day (7 am to 7 pm) and 30 decibels at night (7 pm to 7 am). These limits apply to both indoor and outdoor noise. If you exceed the limit, your neighbors can file a complaint with the municipality, and you may face a fine.

It's important to note that these limits are not absolute. The municipality will take into account the time of day, the type of noise, and the duration of the noise. Commercial Areas

If you live in a commercial area, the noise regulations are less strict than in residential areas. The noise limit is 50 decibels during the day (7 am to 7 pm) and 45 decibels at night (7 pm to 7 am). These limits apply to both indoor and outdoor noise.

Public Events

If you're organizing a public event, such as a festival or concert, you'll need to obtain a permit from the municipality. The permit will include noise regulations that you must comply with.
