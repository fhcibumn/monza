Washington Hall: The Pride Of Radford University

Radford University is known for its beautiful campus and state-of-the-art facilities. Among them, Washington Hall is one of the most iconic buildings on campus. Built in 1939, it has a rich history and has been a hub of activity for students and faculty alike.

Originally built as a men's dormitory, Washington Hall has undergone several renovations over the years. It now houses several academic departments, including the Department of Psychology and the School of Communication. The building also contains a lecture hall, classrooms, and offices for faculty and staff.

Theatre and Performing Arts

One of the main features of Washington Hall is its theatre and performing arts facilities. The Pridemore Playhouse, located in the basement of the building, is a 200-seat theatre that hosts a variety of productions throughout the year. The playhouse is home to the university's Department of Theatre and Cinema, which offers a wide range of programs in theatre arts, film studies, and production.

Washington Hall also houses the Performance Hall, a 500-seat auditorium that is used for concerts, lectures, and other events. The hall is equipped with state-of-the-art sound and lighting systems, making it a premier venue for performing arts on campus. Students in the School of Music also use the Performance Hall for recitals and concerts.

Academic Excellence

As mentioned earlier, Washington Hall houses several academic departments. The Department of Psychology is one of the largest departments in the building, offering undergraduate and graduate programs in psychology, counseling, and human development. The department is known for its research in areas such as addiction, trauma, and social behavior.

The School of Communication is another prominent department in Washington Hall, offering programs in communication studies, journalism, and public relations. Students in the school have access to state-of-the-art facilities, including a newsroom, a television studio, and a radio station. The school also hosts a variety of events and workshops throughout the year, bringing in industry professionals to speak with students.

A Historic Landmark

Aside from its academic and performing arts facilities, Washington Hall is also a historic landmark. The building has been a part of Radford University's history for over 80 years, and many alumni have fond memories of their time spent there. In 2002, the building was added to the National Register of Historic Places, recognizing its significance in the history of the university and the state of Virginia.

Today, Washington Hall continues to be a hub of activity on campus. Its rich history, state-of-the-art facilities, and academic excellence make it a source of pride for Radford University and its community.
