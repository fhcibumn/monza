How to Propagate Lamb's Ear

Lamb's ear is a perennial herb with soft, fuzzy leaves that are silver or green in color. It is known for its low maintenance and ability to attract bees and butterflies to the garden. Propagating lamb's ear is a simple process that can be done in a few different ways. In this article, we will go over four methods for propagating lamb's ear and how to care for the new plants.

Division

One of the easiest ways to propagate lamb's ear is through division. This method should be done in the spring or fall when the plant is not in bloom. Simply dig up the entire plant and gently separate the root ball into smaller sections. Replant the new sections in a well-draining soil mix and water thoroughly. The new plants should establish themselves quickly and start to grow within a few weeks.

Root Cuttings

Another way to propagate lamb's ear is through root cuttings. This method should be done in the fall or winter when the plant is dormant. Cut a few sections of the root from the parent plant and plant them in a well-draining soil mix. Water the cuttings thoroughly and cover them with a plastic bag to create a mini greenhouse. Keep the soil moist and in a warm, well-lit area until the cuttings establish roots. Once they have rooted, remove the plastic bag and care for them as you would any other lamb's ear plant.

Leaf Cuttings

Leaf cuttings are another way to propagate lamb's ear, but this method is less reliable than the others. Simply cut off a few leaves from the parent plant and dip the cut end in rooting hormone. Plant the cuttings in a well-draining soil mix and water thoroughly. Cover the cuttings with a plastic bag to create a mini greenhouse and keep the soil moist in a warm, well-lit area. The cuttings should root within a few weeks, but not all of them will survive.

Seed Propagation

Finally, lamb's ear can also be propagated through seeds. Collect the seeds in the fall and plant them in a well-draining soil mix in the spring. Cover the seeds lightly with soil and water thoroughly. Keep the soil moist and in a warm, well-lit area until the seeds germinate. Once the new plants have established themselves, they can be transplanted to their permanent location in the garden.

Regardless of the method you choose, it is important to care for the new plants after propagation. Water them regularly and make sure they are planted in a well-draining soil mix.
