How Many Floors in Burj Khalifa?

When it comes to iconic buildings, the Burj Khalifa in Dubai is definitely a standout. Completed in 2010, this skyscraper is the tallest building in the world, standing at an incredible height of 828 meters. It's no wonder that visitors from all over the world come to see this impressive structure. But do you know how many floors are in the Burj Khalifa?

Well, the answer is not as simple as you might expect. The Burj Khalifa has a total of 163 floors, but only 154 of those are above ground level. The other nine floors are located below ground, including a parking garage and mechanical rooms. The building also has a whopping 57 elevators and eight escalators to help move people around.

Each floor of the Burj Khalifa serves a different purpose. The lower floors are primarily used for commercial and office space, with companies such as Ernst & Young and Samsung having offices there. The higher floors, on the other hand, are used for residential purposes. The building features luxurious apartments, including a triplex penthouse that spans three floors and has its own private pool.

The Observation Deck

One of the most popular attractions in the Burj Khalifa is the observation deck, located on the 124th floor. From here, visitors can enjoy stunning views of Dubai and beyond. The observation deck is accessed via a high-speed elevator that travels at a speed of 10 meters per second. Once you reach the top, you'll be able to see for miles around.

The Burj Khalifa at Night

If you're lucky enough to visit the Burj Khalifa at night, you're in for a real treat. The building is illuminated by a spectacular light show that uses over 1.2 million LED lights. The lights are controlled by a sophisticated computer system that can create a variety of patterns and colors. It's a truly magical sight that you won't soon forget.

The Burj Khalifa in Movies

The Burj Khalifa has been featured in several movies, including "Mission: Impossible - Ghost Protocol" and "Fast & Furious 7." In "Mission: Impossible," Tom Cruise famously scaled the building's exterior using suction gloves. While this scene was obviously fictional, it's still an impressive feat that shows just how iconic the Burj Khalifa has become.

The Burj Khalifa as a Symbol of Dubai

The Burj Khalifa is not just a stunning piece of architecture; it's also become a symbol of Dubai itself. The building represents the city's ambition, wealth, and modernity. It's a testament to what can be achieved when people work together towards a common goal. The Burj Khalifa is a source of pride for the people of Dubai and a must-see attraction for visitors to the city.
