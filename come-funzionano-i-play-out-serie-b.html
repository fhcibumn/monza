Introduction

Play-out is a system that determines which teams will be relegated from Serie B to Serie C. It is a playoff between the teams that finished in the bottom four of the Serie B table. The two teams that finish at the bottom of the table are automatically relegated to Serie C, while the other two teams play-off against each other to determine who will stay in Serie B.

1. Play-out Format

The play-out system in Serie B is a two-legged playoff between the third-bottom and fourth-bottom teams in the league table. The team that finishes third-bottom will play the first leg at home, while the team that finishes fourth-bottom will play the second leg at home. The team with the highest aggregate score after the two legs will stay in Serie B, while the other team will be relegated to Serie C.

The play-out matches are usually played in May or June, after the regular season has ended. The dates for the play-out matches are announced by the Italian Football Federation (FIGC) a few weeks before the matches are scheduled to take place.

The teams that are relegated from Serie B are replaced by the top three teams from Serie C. The team that finishes in first place in Serie C is automatically promoted to Serie B, while the teams that finish in second and third place play-off against each other to determine the second team that will be promoted to Serie B.

On the other hand, the team that wins the play-out in Serie B stays in the league for another season. The team that loses the play-out is relegated to Serie C, where they will have to play in the lower division for the following season.

The play-out system was introduced in Serie B in the 2003-2004 season. The play-out system is an important part of Italian football, especially in Serie B.
