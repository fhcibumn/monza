What Caused George to Stop Playing Mean-Spirited Jokes on Lennie?

It's been a few years since George and Lennie's story was first introduced to us in John Steinbeck's classic novel, "Of Mice and Men." We all remember the dynamic between the two characters - George, the sharp-witted and practical one, and Lennie, the gentle giant with a childlike mind. One of the recurring themes in the book was George's tendency to play mean-spirited jokes on Lennie. However, as the story progresses, we see a change in George's behavior towards his friend. But what caused him to stop playing these jokes? Let's explore.

Realization of Lennie's Vulnerability

One of the reasons why George may have stopped playing mean-spirited jokes on Lennie is because he came to realize how vulnerable Lennie was. Lennie, with his childlike mind, was unable to fully comprehend the consequences of his actions. He would often unintentionally cause harm to others, which would put him in danger. George, being the caretaker and friend that he was, may have realized that it was his responsibility to protect Lennie from harm, both physical and emotional. Playing jokes on Lennie could have potentially caused him distress, and George may have realized that it was not worth the risk.

Empathy and Understanding

As the story progresses, we see George develop a deeper understanding of Lennie's condition. He begins to see him not just as a burden, but as a person with feelings and emotions. This empathy and understanding may have led George to stop playing mean-spirited jokes on Lennie. He may have realized that Lennie was already struggling with the challenges of his condition, and that playing jokes on him would only add to his difficulties. Instead, George may have shifted his focus to finding ways to make Lennie's life easier and more comfortable.

Growth and Maturity

Another reason why George may have stopped playing mean-spirited jokes on Lennie is due to personal growth and maturity. As the story progresses, we see George evolve from a self-centered and impulsive person to a responsible and caring friend. He begins to take on the role of a protector and mentor to Lennie, and this growth may have led him to reevaluate his behavior towards his friend. Playing jokes on Lennie may have been something that he did in the past without fully considering the impact it had on his friend. But as he grew and matured, he may have realized that it was not the right way to treat someone he cared about.

Respect and Love

Finally, George's decision to stop playing mean-spirited jokes on Lennie may have simply been due to his respect and love for his friend. Despite Lennie's challenges and shortcomings, George recognized that he was a person deserving of respect and kindness. Playing jokes on him may have been a way to assert dominance or control, but as George's friendship with Lennie deepened, he may have realized that this was not the way to treat someone he loved. Instead, he may have chosen to show Lennie the respect and care that he deserved, and to treat him as an equal.
