Mary Berry's Fish Pie - A Delicious Classic

Introduction

Mary Berry's Fish Pie is a classic British dish that has been enjoyed by families for generations. This creamy and indulgent pie is filled with tender fish, vegetables, and topped with a buttery mashed potato crust. It's a perfect comfort food for any occasion, whether it's a weekday dinner or a special occasion.

In this article, we'll take a closer look at Mary Berry's Fish Pie and explore some of the reasons why it has remained a beloved dish in the UK for so long.

The History of Mary Berry's Fish Pie

Mary Berry is a well-respected British chef and cookbook author who has been cooking for over 60 years. Her Fish Pie recipe has become one of her most famous dishes, loved by home cooks and professional chefs alike.

The origin of Fish Pie can be traced back to the 16th century, where it was known as "Fisherman's Pie." It was a simple dish made by fishermen's wives using whatever fish they had caught that day, along with mashed potatoes and other ingredients.

Over time, the dish evolved and became more sophisticated. Mary Berry's recipe uses a combination of smoked haddock, salmon, and prawns, along with a creamy sauce made with milk, cream, and white wine. It's topped with a buttery mashed potato crust and finished off with a sprinkle of grated cheese.

The Ingredients

The key to a delicious Fish Pie is using high-quality ingredients. Mary Berry's recipe calls for a combination of smoked haddock, salmon, and prawns. These three types of fish work together to create a rich and complex flavor.

The sauce is made with butter, flour, milk, cream, and white wine. This creates a creamy and indulgent sauce that complements the fish perfectly. The mashed potato topping is made with butter and cream, making it rich and buttery.

Other ingredients in the recipe include leeks, carrots, celery, and parsley. These vegetables add flavor and nutrition to the dish, making it a well-rounded meal.

How to Make Mary Berry's Fish Pie

While Mary Berry's Fish Pie may seem like a complicated dish, it's actually quite easy to make. The key is to prepare all the ingredients in advance so that you can assemble the pie quickly.

First, you need to poach the fish in milk. This helps to infuse the fish with flavor and makes it tender. Then, you make the sauce and vegetables and layer them with the fish in a baking dish. Finally, you top the pie with mashed potatoes and bake it in the oven until it's golden brown and bubbling.

Once you've made Mary Berry's Fish Pie, you'll understand why it's such a beloved dish in the UK. It's a comforting and indulgent meal that's perfect for any occasion.
