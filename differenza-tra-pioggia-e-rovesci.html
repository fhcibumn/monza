Pioggia

Pioggia is the Italian word for "rain," and it typically refers to a steady, continuous rainfall. This is the kind of rain that falls gently from the sky, creating a soothing pitter-patter on the roof. It's the kind of rain that makes you want to curl up with a good book and a cup of tea.

Rovesci

Rovesci, on the other hand, is a much more intense form of rainfall. This term is often used to describe a sudden, heavy downpour that comes out of nowhere. Rovesci can be accompanied by thunder and lightning, and it can be quite scary if you're caught in it unexpectedly.

Rovesci can also be dangerous if you're driving, as it can create poor visibility and slippery roads. It's important to pull over and wait out the storm if you find yourself in this situation.
