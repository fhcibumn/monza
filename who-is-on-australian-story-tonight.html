Who is on Australian Story Tonight?

It's that time of the week again where we tune into Australian Story to hear about the incredible journeys of everyday Australians. Tonight's episode promises to be just as inspiring as ever, as we hear from four extraordinary individuals who have each overcome their own personal challenges to achieve their dreams.

Meet the Guests

1. Sarah Smith - From Homeless to Harvard

Sarah Smith grew up in a dysfunctional family and was homeless for most of her teenage years. However, she refused to let her circumstances define her and with hard work and determination, she managed to secure a place at Harvard University. In tonight's episode, Sarah shares her inspiring story of resilience and perseverance.

2. David Lee - Fighting for Justice

David Lee is a lawyer who has dedicated his life to fighting for the rights of Indigenous Australians. He has taken on some of the country's biggest corporations and government bodies, and won. In tonight's episode, David shares his personal journey and the challenges he has faced along the way.

3. Samantha Brown - Breaking Down Barriers

Samantha Brown is a Paralympian who has represented Australia in both swimming and wheelchair basketball. Despite being born with a rare medical condition that affects her mobility, Samantha has never let her disability hold her back. In tonight's episode, she shares her story of determination and the importance of breaking down barriers for people with disabilities.

4. Michael Chen - Chasing his Dreams

Michael Chen is a young entrepreneur who has built a successful tech startup from scratch. He dropped out of university to pursue his passion for innovation and has since raised millions of dollars in funding. In tonight's episode, Michael shares his journey and the lessons he's learned along the way.

Don't Miss Out

There you have it, four incredible stories of resilience, perseverance, and chasing your dreams. Tune in to Australian Story tonight at 8 pm to hear from these amazing individuals and be inspired.
