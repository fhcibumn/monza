Funny NFL Draft Moments

The NFL Draft is an exciting time for football fans all over the world. It's a chance for teams to select the best young talent to help lead them to future success. However, with all the pressure and expectations, there are bound to be some hilarious and memorable moments that occur during the draft. Here are just a few of the funniest moments in NFL Draft history.

The Time the Seahawks Forgot to Make Their Pick

In 2019, the Seattle Seahawks were on the clock for their selection when they seemingly forgot to make their pick. The clock ran out, and the next team was allowed to make their selection. It was later revealed that the Seahawks had in fact submitted their pick, but due to technical difficulties, it was not received by the league office in time. This mishap led to plenty of jokes and memes on social media.

The Time Aaron Rodgers Was Passed Up

In 2005, Aaron Rodgers was expected to be a top pick in the draft. However, as teams made their selections, Rodgers continued to slide down the board. When the Green Bay Packers finally selected him with the 24th pick, cameras caught Rodgers looking less than thrilled. This moment has since become a meme and a reminder to never give up on your dreams, no matter how many times you get passed up.

The Time the Cowboys Drafted a Player Who Was Already Dead

In 1992, the Dallas Cowboys selected defensive tackle Leon Lett in the seventh round of the draft. However, it was later discovered that Lett had passed away two months prior due to a cocaine overdose. The Cowboys were understandably embarrassed by the mistake and quickly released a statement apologizing for the error.

The Time Deion Sanders Wore a Flashy Outfit to the Draft

Deion Sanders is known for his flashy style both on and off the field. So, it's no surprise that he showed up to the 1989 NFL Draft wearing a bright red suit, gold chain, and sunglasses. His outfit caused quite a stir and has since become an iconic moment in draft history.

The Time the Broncos Accidentally Drafted the Wrong Player

In 2018, the Denver Broncos were on the clock and thought they had selected tight end Jake Butt out of Michigan. However, when they went to announce the pick, they accidentally said the name of offensive tackle Garett Bolles instead. It was quickly corrected, but the moment lives on as a reminder to always double check your selections.

The Time Johnny Manziel Sent a Text to the Browns Begging to be Drafted

In 2014, quarterback Johnny Manziel was hoping to be selected early in the draft. However, as the picks continued to roll in, Manziel started to get nervous. He reportedly sent a text to the Cleveland Browns pleading with them to select him. The Browns eventually did pick him, but it's unclear if the text had any influence on their decision.
