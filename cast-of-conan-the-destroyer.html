Cast Of Conan The Destroyer: A Look Back At The Iconic Film

In 1984, the world was introduced to "Conan the Destroyer," the sequel to the 1982 film "Conan the Barbarian." Directed by Richard Fleischer, the film starred Arnold Schwarzenegger in the titular role and boasted an impressive cast of supporting actors. Let's take a look back at the cast of "Conan the Destroyer" and what they brought to the film.

Grace Jones as Zula

One of the standouts of the cast is Grace Jones, who played the fierce warrior Zula. Jones, known for her work as a singer and model, brought a unique energy to the role. Zula is a powerful fighter who becomes Conan's ally, and Jones' physicality and charisma added depth to the character. Her performance in the film helped solidify her as a pop culture icon.

Wilt Chamberlain as Bombaata

Another memorable performance in "Conan the Destroyer" comes from basketball legend Wilt Chamberlain, who played Bombaata, the captain of Queen Taramis' guard. Chamberlain's imposing stature made him a perfect fit for the role, and his performance showcased his acting ability. Bombaata is a complex character, torn between his duty to the queen and his growing respect for Conan. Chamberlain's portrayal added depth to the character and helped make him a fan favorite.

Olivia d'Abo as Princess Jehnna

Olivia d'Abo played Princess Jehnna, the young woman who recruits Conan and his allies to help her retrieve a magical horn. D'Abo's performance added a sense of vulnerability to the character, as she struggles to reconcile her duty as a princess with her desire for adventure. Her chemistry with Schwarzenegger helped make their scenes together some of the film's best.

Mako as Akiro the Wizard

Mako played Akiro the Wizard, a character he had previously portrayed in "Conan the Barbarian." Akiro is a trusted ally of Conan's, using his magic to aid the group on their quest. Mako's performance added a sense of humor to the film, and his scenes provided a welcome break from the action. His chemistry with Schwarzenegger was also a highlight of the film.
