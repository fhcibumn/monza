Vegetables in Season UK - News Blog 2023

Why Eating Seasonally is Important

Eating seasonally means consuming fruits and vegetables that are grown and harvested during their natural season. This is beneficial for several reasons. Firstly, seasonal produce is fresher and has a better taste. Secondly, it is more environmentally friendly as it reduces the need for long-distance transportation. Thirdly, it is usually cheaper as there is an abundance of supply during the peak season. By eating seasonally, you can support local farmers and enjoy the best produce that nature has to offer.

Spring Vegetables in Season

Spring is the time for fresh, vibrant and tender vegetables. Some of the vegetables in season during spring in the UK include asparagus, broccoli, cauliflower, kale, spinach, and radishes. These vegetables are packed with nutrients and are perfect for making light and healthy meals. You can enjoy them in salads, stir-fries, soups, or roasted with some olive oil and seasoning.

Summer Vegetables in Season

Summer is the season for juicy and refreshing vegetables. Some of the vegetables in season during summer in the UK include courgettes, tomatoes, cucumbers, peppers, aubergines, and beans. These vegetables are perfect for making refreshing salads, grilled kebabs, or stir-fries. You can also enjoy them as a side dish or as a snack with some hummus or tzatziki.

Autumn Vegetables in Season

Autumn is the time for hearty and warming vegetables. Some of the vegetables in season during autumn in the UK include pumpkins, squash, sweet potatoes, carrots, parsnips, and Brussels sprouts. These vegetables are perfect for making comforting soups, stews, and roasted dishes. You can also enjoy them as a side dish or as a main course with some whole grains or protein.

Winter Vegetables in Season

Winter is the season for robust and flavorful vegetables. Some of the vegetables in season during winter in the UK include cabbage, leeks, onions, garlic, beetroot, and turnips. These vegetables are perfect for making hearty soups, stews, and casseroles. You can also enjoy them as a side dish or as a main course with some potatoes or meat.
