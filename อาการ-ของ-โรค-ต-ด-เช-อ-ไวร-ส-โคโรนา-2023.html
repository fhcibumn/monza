Fever and Cough

The most common symptoms of COVID-19 are fever and cough. In 2023, these symptoms will still be prevalent, and it is important to monitor your body temperature and any persistent coughing. If you have a fever of 100.4°F or higher, you should seek medical attention immediately. It is also important to note that some people may not experience a fever, but still have the virus.

If you have a cough that lasts for more than a week, or if you experience shortness of breath, you should also seek medical attention. COVID-19 can cause respiratory problems, and it is important to monitor your breathing and seek help if necessary.

Loss of Taste and Smell

In 2023, loss of taste and smell may still be a symptom of COVID-19. This can make it difficult to enjoy food and beverages, and may also impact your ability to detect dangerous odors. If you experience a sudden loss of taste or smell, you should get tested for COVID-19 and isolate yourself until you receive your results.

Loss of taste and smell may also be accompanied by other symptoms, such as fatigue, headaches, and muscle aches. If you experience any of these symptoms, you should seek medical attention.

Gastrointestinal Symptoms

In addition to respiratory symptoms, COVID-19 can also cause gastrointestinal symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. These symptoms may be more common in children, but can also affect adults. If you experience gastrointestinal symptoms along with respiratory symptoms, you should seek medical attention.

Gastrointestinal symptoms may also be accompanied by a loss of appetite and abdominal pain. It is important to stay hydrated and seek medical attention if your symptoms persist for more than a few days.

Mental Health Symptoms

The COVID-19 pandemic has taken a toll on mental health, and in 2023, mental health symptoms may still be prevalent. Anxiety, depression, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) may all be associated with COVID-19, especially for those who have experienced severe illness or loss.

If you are experiencing mental health symptoms, it is important to seek support and treatment. This can include speaking with a mental health professional, joining a support group, or practicing self-care techniques such as meditation and exercise.
