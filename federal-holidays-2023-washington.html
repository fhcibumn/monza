Federal Holidays 2023 in Washington

Celebrate the New Year with a Federal Holiday

Washington residents can start 2023 off on the right foot with a federal holiday on January 2. This day is observed on the first Monday in January and gives workers a much-needed break after the busy holiday season. Take this opportunity to relax and spend time with loved ones, or explore the many attractions Washington has to offer. Enjoy the winter wonderland of Leavenworth, take a trip to the Olympic Peninsula, or visit the iconic Space Needle in Seattle.

Remembering the Life and Legacy of Martin Luther King Jr.

Martin Luther King Jr. Day is observed on January 16 in 2023. This federal holiday honors the civil rights leader who dedicated his life to advocating for racial equality and social justice. Many organizations and communities hold events and activities to celebrate King's legacy, including marches, speeches, and volunteer opportunities. Take part in a local event to honor King's message of love and unity.

Reflecting on the Sacrifices of Presidents' Day

Presidents' Day is observed on February 20 in 2023. This federal holiday honors the birthdays of George Washington and Abraham Lincoln, two of America's most iconic presidents. While it's a day off work for many, it's also a time to reflect on the sacrifices and contributions of past leaders. Visit the National Mall and pay respects to the Lincoln Memorial and Washington Monument, or explore the many museums and historical sites around the city.

Enjoying the Beauty of Memorial Day

Memorial Day is observed on May 29 in 2023. This federal holiday is dedicated to honoring the men and women who died while serving in the U.S. military. It's also the unofficial start of summer, and many people take advantage of the long weekend to travel or spend time outdoors. Visit one of Washington's many parks or beaches, or attend a Memorial Day parade or ceremony to pay your respects.

Celebrating Independence Day in the Nation's Capital

Independence Day is observed on July 4 in 2023. This federal holiday celebrates the signing of the Declaration of Independence and the birth of American independence. Washington, D.C. is a popular destination for Fourth of July celebrations, with events like the National Independence Day Parade, the Capitol Fourth concert, and fireworks over the National Mall. Join the festivities and show your patriotic spirit!
