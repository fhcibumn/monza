Quotes from Fahrenheit 451 with Page Numbers

In Ray Bradbury's Fahrenheit 451, the author paints a dystopian future where books are banned, and firefighters start fires rather than extinguishing them. The novel is a cautionary tale about the dangers of censorship and the importance of knowledge. Here are some quotes from Fahrenheit 451 with page numbers that highlight the themes of the book.

The Danger of Censorship

"With schools turning out more runners, jumpers, racers, tinkerers, grabbers, snatchers, fliers, and swimmers instead of examiners, critics, knowers, and imaginative creators, the word 'intellectual,' of course, became the swear word it deserved to be." (pg. 58)

This quote underscores the danger of censorship and how it can lead to a society that values physical activity over intellectual pursuits. It shows how intellectualism is viewed as a negative trait and how society has lost its appreciation for knowledge and imagination.

"It was a pleasure to burn. It was a special pleasure to see things eaten, to see things blackened and changed." (pg. 1)

This quote shows how the firefighters in the novel take pleasure in burning books and destroying knowledge. It highlights the danger of censorship and how it can lead to a society that is ignorant and unable to think critically.

"We have everything we need to be happy, but we aren't happy. Something's missing." (pg. 58)

This quote shows how censorship and the suppression of knowledge can lead to a society that is unhappy and unfulfilled. It highlights the importance of knowledge and how it can enrich our lives and bring us happiness.

The Importance of Knowledge

Ignorance

"But remember that the Captain belongs to the most dangerous enemy of truth and freedom, the solid unmoving cattle of the majority." (pg. 61)

This quote shows how the majority can be dangerous to truth and freedom. It highlights the importance of knowledge and the danger of ignorance. It shows how a society that values conformity over individuality can be destructive and oppressive.

Individuality

"We need not to be let alone. We need to be really bothered once in a while. How long is it since you were really bothered? About something important, about something real?" (pg. 51)

This quote shows how important it is to be bothered and to question things. It highlights the importance of individuality and how a society that values conformity over individuality can be oppressive. It shows how knowledge can lead to personal growth and development.

Curiosity

"I'm seventeen and I'm crazy. My uncle says the two always go together. When people ask your age, he said, always say seventeen and insane." (pg. 13)

This quote shows how curiosity and a willingness to question things can lead to personal growth and development. It highlights the importance of knowledge and how it can lead to a deeper understanding of ourselves and the world around us.

The Power of Books

"There must be something in books, things we can't imagine, to make a woman stay in a burning house; there must be something there. You don't stay for nothing." (pg. 48)

This quote shows how books can have a powerful and transformative effect on our lives. It highlights the importance of knowledge and how it can change our perspectives and our understanding of the world around us.

"We cannot tell the precise moment when friendship is formed. As in filling a vessel drop by drop, there is at last a drop which makes it run over; so in a series of kindnesses there is at last one which makes the heart run over. (pg. 155)

This quote shows how books can teach us about the importance of friendship and human connection. It highlights the transformative power of literature and how it can help us to understand ourselves and those around us.

"A book is a loaded gun in the house next door. Burn it. Take the shot from the weapon. Breach man's mind." (pg. 58)

This quote shows how books can be seen as dangerous and subversive. It highlights the power of knowledge and the fear that those in power may have of it. It shows how books can be a force for change and how they can inspire people to think critically and question the status quo.
